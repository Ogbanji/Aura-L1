Governance Overview

AURA-L1 is governed by its community through an on-chain DAO framework, enabling management of critical protocol parameters while maintaining safety, transparency, and upgrade flexibility.

The governance layer is modular, allowing future enhancements or additional governance mechanisms without disrupting core protocol logic.

⸻

Governance Objectives
	•	Encourage community ownership and participation
	•	Ensure transparent and auditable decision-making
	•	Support safe protocol evolution with minimal disruption

⸻

Participants

Governance involves multiple stakeholders:
	•	Validators – responsible for protocol security and proposal validation
	•	Token holders – participate in voting proportional to stake
	•	Delegates – may vote on behalf of token holders via delegation mechanisms

The active participant set may evolve based on governance decisions and stake distribution.

⸻

Proposal Types

Governance supports a variety of protocol actions:
	•	Chain parameter updates
	•	Validator registration or removal
	•	Protocol upgrades and module replacements
	•	Treasury allocations and spending decisions

Each proposal type follows defined review and voting procedures.

⸻

Voting Process

The governance process follows a structured flow:
	1.	Proposal submission
	2.	Review and discussion period
	3.	Voting period (stake-weighted)
	4.	Execution upon approval

All votes are recorded on-chain to ensure auditability and transparency.
